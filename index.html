Banking · Deposits & Loans

Accounts

Each account rolls into the statement automatically by its type and entity: personal deposits into Cash & Equivalents, business deposits into Business Accounts, loans and credit lines into Liabilities. Click any account to edit details or add loan terms.

Income & Expenses

Cash Flow

By entity

Income uses active monthly rent and trailing-12m vehicle rental income (÷12). Debt service is your scheduled monthly payments. Property taxes are annual ÷12. All figures are based on current data — update account balances and lease terms to keep this accurate.

By entity & business

Entities

Each card rolls up all accounts and properties tagged to that entity. Cash flow = active monthly rent minus all debt service payments. Health score weighs cash-flow positivity, leverage ratio, and account balance.

Policies & coverage

Insurance

Each policy can cover one or more properties or vehicles. The coverage gap panel flags any asset not matched to an active policy by name or address. Policies expiring within 90 days show in amber; expired policies show in red.

Amortization & payoff

Loans

Projections assume the current balance, payment, and rate hold steady — extra principal payments shorten the schedule. Where the payment only covers interest (lines of credit), no payoff date exists by design. Add missing rates in each property's editor to unlock its projection.

Investment holdings

Portfolio

Combined positions — all accounts

Holdings are imported from brokerage statements and reflect the statement date, not live prices. Update by sending a new statement to Claude. Positions under $50 are grouped as "small positions."

2nd Chance Motors · Chance Classics

Vehicle Fleet

Vehicle values roll into the balance sheet as physical assets under the 2nd Chance entity. Rental income is tracked separately from property income. Click any vehicle to edit details.

Tracked Over Time

Net Worth Trend

Each snapshot freezes your full financial composition — assets, debt, cash, property, investments, vehicles — so the trend can show what drove each change. Save one whenever a balance changes — a loan paydown, a new lease, a revalued property — and the trend builds itself.
